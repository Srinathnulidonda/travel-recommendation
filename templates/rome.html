Rome Through the Ages

753 BC

Founding of Rome: According to legend, Romulus founds Rome on the Palatine Hill.

509 BC

Roman Republic: The last Etruscan king is overthrown, and the Roman Republic is established.

27 BC - 476 AD

Roman Empire: Augustus becomes the first Roman Emperor, marking the beginning of the Roman Empire.

80 AD

Colosseum Completed: The iconic Flavian Amphitheater, known as the Colosseum, is inaugurated.

313 AD

Edict of Milan: Emperor Constantine I legalizes Christianity in the Roman Empire.

1377

Return of the Papacy: The Papacy returns to Rome from Avignon, France.

1508-1512

Sistine Chapel: Michelangelo paints the ceiling of the Sistine Chapel.

1626

St. Peter's Basilica: The new St. Peter's Basilica is completed and consecrated.

1870

Unification of Italy: Rome becomes the capital of the newly unified Kingdom of Italy.

1929

Vatican City: The Lateran Treaty establishes Vatican City as an independent state.

1980

UNESCO World Heritage: The historic center of Rome is designated as a UNESCO World Heritage site.

Colosseum

About the Colosseum

The Colosseum, also known as the Flavian Amphitheatre, is an oval amphitheatre in the centre of the city of Rome, Italy. Built of travertine limestone, tuff (volcanic rock), and brick-faced concrete, it was the largest amphitheatre ever built at the time and held 50,000 to 80,000 spectators.

Highlights:
  • Iconic symbol of Imperial Rome
  • Architectural marvel of the ancient world
  • Venue for gladiatorial contests and public spectacles
  • Underground chambers where gladiators and animals were kept
Visitor Information:
  • Opening Hours: 8:30 AM - 7:00 PM (last entry at 6:00 PM)
  • Address: Piazza del Colosseo, 1, 00184 Roma RM, Italy
  • Nearest Metro: Colosseo (Line B)

Trevi Fountain

About Trevi Fountain

The Trevi Fountain is a fountain in the Trevi district in Rome, Italy, designed by Italian architect Nicola Salvi and completed by Giuseppe Pannini and several others. Standing 26.3 metres (86 ft) high and 49.15 metres (161.3 ft) wide, it is the largest Baroque fountain in the city and one of the most famous fountains in the world.

Highlights:
  • Iconic Baroque architecture
  • Coin tossing tradition
  • Beautiful night illumination
  • Central figure of Oceanus
Visitor Information:
  • Open 24 hours
  • Address: Piazza di Trevi, 00187 Roma RM, Italy
  • Nearest Metro: Barberini (Line A)

Roman Forum

About the Roman Forum

The Roman Forum, also known by its Latin name Forum Romanum, is a rectangular forum surrounded by the ruins of several important ancient government buildings at the center of the city of Rome. Citizens of the ancient city referred to this space, originally a marketplace, as the Forum Magnum, or simply the Forum.

Highlights:
  • Temple of Saturn
  • Arch of Titus
  • Temple of Vesta
  • Basilica Julia
Visitor Information:
  • Opening Hours: 8:30 AM - 7:00 PM (last entry 1 hour before closing)
  • Address: Via della Salara Vecchia, 5/6, 00186 Roma RM, Italy
  • Nearest Metro: Colosseo (Line B)

Pantheon

About the Pantheon

The Pantheon is a former Roman temple and since the year 609 a Catholic church, in Rome, Italy, on the site of an earlier temple commissioned by Marcus Agrippa during the reign of Augustus. It was rebuilt by the emperor Hadrian and probably dedicated c. 126 AD. Its date of construction is uncertain, because Hadrian chose not to inscribe the new temple but rather to retain the inscription of Agrippa's older temple, which had burned down.

Highlights:
  • Massive unreinforced concrete dome
  • Oculus (central opening to the sky)
  • Ancient Roman architecture
  • Tombs of Italian kings and the artist Raphael
Visitor Information:
  • Opening Hours: 9:00 AM - 7:00 PM (last entry at 6:30 PM)
  • Address: Piazza della Rotonda, 00186 Roma RM, Italy
  • Nearest Metro: Barberini (Line A)
