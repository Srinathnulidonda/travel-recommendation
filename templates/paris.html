Eiffel Tower
The Icon of Paris

The Eiffel Tower is a wrought-iron lattice tower on the Champ de Mars in Paris. Constructed from 1887 to 1889, it has become the most iconic symbol of both Paris and France.

Quick Facts:
  • 🏗️ Height: 324 meters
  • 📅 Opened: March 31, 1889
  • 👥 Annual Visitors: 7 million
  • 🎨 Paint Used: 60 tons every 7 years
Best Times to Visit:
  • 🌅 Sunrise: Peaceful views
  • 🌆 Sunset: Romantic atmosphere
  • ✨ Night: Sparkling light show
Louvre Museum
World's Largest Art Museum

The Louvre Museum is the world's largest art museum and a historic monument in Paris. Home to thousands of works of art, including the famous Mona Lisa, it's a must-visit destination for art lovers.

Highlights:
  • 🎨 Mona Lisa
  • 🗽 Venus de Milo
  • 👑 Crown Jewels
  • ⚔️ Ancient Artifacts
Visitor Information:
  • ⏰ Hours: 9 AM - 6 PM
  • 📅 Closed: Tuesdays
  • 🎟️ Free: Under 18
Notre-Dame Cathedral
Medieval Catholic Cathedral

Notre-Dame de Paris is a medieval Catholic cathedral on the Île de la Cité. Known for its French Gothic architecture, the cathedral is currently undergoing restoration following the 2019 fire.

Architecture:
  • 🏰 Style: French Gothic
  • 📏 Height: 69 meters
  • 🎨 Famous Gargoyles
Restoration:
  • 🏗️ Ongoing Repairs
  • 📅 Expected Completion: 2024
  • 🔄 Historic Preservation
Arc de Triomphe
Iconic Monument of Paris

The Arc de Triomphe honors those who fought and died for France in the French Revolutionary and Napoleonic Wars. The monument stands at the western end of the Champs-Élysées at the center of Place Charles de Gaulle.

Quick Facts:
  • 🏛️ Height: 50 meters
  • 📅 Completed: 1836
  • 🔥 Eternal flame: Tomb of the Unknown Soldier
Visitor Information:
  • 🕒 Open daily: 10 AM - 10:30 PM
  • 🎟️ Admission: Paid entry
  • 🏙️ Panoramic views of Paris
Sacré-Cœur
The Sacred Heart of Paris

The Basilica of the Sacred Heart of Paris, commonly known as Sacré-Cœur Basilica, is a Roman Catholic church and minor basilica in Paris, France. A popular landmark, it is located at the summit of the butte Montmartre, the highest point in the city.

Architecture:
  • 🏛️ Style: Romano-Byzantine
  • 📅 Completed: 1914
  • 🔔 One of the largest bells in France
Visitor Information:
  • 🕒 Open daily: 6:30 AM - 10:30 PM
  • 🎟️ Free entry to the Basilica
  • 🏙️ Panoramic view of Paris
Palace of Versailles
Royal Château of Versailles

The Palace of Versailles was the principal royal residence of France from 1682, under Louis XIV, until the start of the French Revolution in 1789, under Louis XVI. It is located in the department of Yvelines, in the region of Île-de-France, about 20 kilometers southwest of the center of Paris.

Highlights:
  • 👑 Hall of Mirrors
  • 🏛️ Royal Opera
  • 🌳 Gardens of Versailles
Visitor Information:
  • 🕒 Open Tue-Sun: 9 AM - 5:30 PM
  • 🎟️ Admission: Paid entry
  • 🚆 RER C from Paris
Musée d'Orsay
Impressionist and Post-Impressionist Masterpieces

The Musée d'Orsay is a museum in Paris, France, on the Left Bank of the Seine. It is housed in the former Gare d'Orsay, a Beaux-Arts railway station built between 1898 and 1900. The museum holds mainly French art dating from 1848 to 1914, including paintings, sculptures, furniture, and photography.

Famous Works:
  • 🎨 Starry Night by Van Gogh
  • 🖼️ Bal du moulin de la Galette by Renoir
  • 🗿 The Gates of Hell by Rodin
Visitor Information:
  • 🕒 Open Tue-Sun: 9:30 AM - 6 PM
  • 🎟️ Admission: Paid entry
  • 🚇 Metro: Solférino
